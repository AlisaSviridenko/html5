Journey to Italy


Bari is a seaside city in Southern Italy, the center of the province of the same name. This is one of the most significant cities in the region, along with Naples, a major tourist and economic center, and a seaport. Bari, like many Italian cities, has a rich, ancient history, so there are many ancient sights and architectural monuments.

The old town of Bari lies on a peninsula between the old and new harbors, and the industrial zone extends inland. There are few tourists, with the exception of Orthodox pilgrims to the relics of St. Nicholas the Wonderworker, which were secretly taken from Myra Lycia in 1087 and have since been kept in the crypt of the Basilica of St. Nicholas specially built to house them (founded in 1087). Since 1915 (approximate date), the Orthodox Church of St. Nicholas the Wonderworker has been operating in Bari, designed in the Old Novgorod spirit by Alexei Shchusev. Services in the church are conducted by a Russian priest of the Moscow Patriarchate. In 2001, a statue of St. Nicholas by Vyacheslav Klykov was installed next to the temple. Other significant buildings in the city include the collegiate church of St. Sabinus, an example of Romanesque architecture (1170–1178, restored in the 1950s), Friedrich Hohenstaufen's seaside castle (Castello Svevo, 13th century) and the ancient Petruzzelli Theater, a renovated building which opened in 2009. What is Bari famous for?

Bari is very famous in the religious community - the relics of St. Nicholas the Wonderworker rest here, which made it one of the centers of the Orthodox Church in Europe.



Districts of Bari

The port city is divided into 11 districts. However, not all of them are attractive to tourists. The city center is Murat, where meetings and appointments are made. This area perfectly connects the city beach with the Old Town. There are a huge number of cafes and restaurants, among which you can find a decent place with real delicious pizza. The largest number of attractions can be found in Bari Vecchia. This area contains the Basilica of St. Nicholas, the Cathedral of Bari and the ruins of the Church of Santa Maria. In the Quartiere Umbertino area there is the Petruzzelli Theater, vinyl and grocery stores where you can buy souvenirs.

ATTRACTIONS

Bari is a wonderful combination of old and new. The modern districts have shopping centers and museums, while the Old Town boasts historic architecture and true Italian flair. A large number of basilicas, churches, temples and other ancient buildings delight all visitors with their beauty. The surrounding area of ​​the city is also worthy of tourists' attention and attracts with its historical attractions.

Population

In total, 622,287 thousand people live in the city. The majority of residents (97%) are Italians, 2% are Albanians and 1% are Greeks and African migrants. In Bari, the number of children under the age of 18 is equal to the number of pensioners living.

Want to know more history of Bari?



During World War II, British divisions captured the city. On the night of December 2, 1943, a tragic event occurred - Bari, the only European city, was subjected to a chemical attack. The events of that night were kept secret until 1967, when writer Glenn B. Infield revealed the story in his book Disaster at Bari. Once again the city suffered a disaster on April 9, 1945, when the Liberty Charles Henderson, carrying a thousand tons of aircraft bombs, exploded in the port. The harbor was destroyed, and human casualties amounted to 360 thousand.
